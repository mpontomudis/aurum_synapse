## 2020 — Crisis / Panic Regime Findings

**Operational failure mode (spread gate).** Early 2020 cohorts exhibited systematic rejection at the spread validation layer: configured **`InpMaxSpreadPoints`** exceeded what the crisis tape routinely permitted under *Every tick based on real ticks*. Resolution: reduce **`InpMaxSpreadPoints`** from **120 → 100** to restore admissible trade generation without loosening unrelated risk controls.

**Tape characteristics (qualitative regime fingerprint).** The 2020 window displayed elevated realized volatility, episodic liquidity stress, persistent spread widening versus calm regimes, unstable continuation after impulse moves, and elevated false-breakout density—conditions where naive continuation logic underperforms unless execution survivability is prioritized.

**Governance interaction (filter stacking).** Strict governance-style stacking (**`RequireTrendAlignment`**, **`RequireKeyLevelProximity`**, **`RequireMomentumConfirmation`** concurrently) materially compressed the executable set. Under crisis conditions this produced **over-constrained** behavior: frequency collapsed while the tape remained discontinuous, amplifying path dependence on sparse fills.

**Architectural conclusion.** Crisis regimes demand **adaptive selectivity** rather than static maximum-precision purity. Over-filtering can destroy opportunity capture when the dominant risk is **non-participation** and **survivability of the execution layer** (spread, halts, path volatility) exceeds marginal gains from additional signal orthogonality.

## CONTROLLED EXPERIMENT MATRIX (2022) — TESTS A THROUGH G

The tests below are described as **archetypes** (pure trend, pure MR, ultra continuation, contextual continuation, adaptive reward extraction, accelerated extraction, aggressive micro-trailing). Each archetype isolates one degree of freedom while holding others as stable as the harness permits, to attribute **which lever moves which moment** of the payoff distribution (mean winner, loser mass, tail risk, drawdown path).

### Test A — Pure Trend (continuation-first, maximal directional permissiveness within governance)

**Design intent.** Maximize exposure to “clean” continuation episodes by enforcing contextual filters and momentum confirmation while keeping continuation-oriented engines active and de-emphasizing mean-reversion participation.

**Observed behavior.**

- Continuation episodes **exist** and validate directionally under the filter stack.
- **Persistence is insufficient** for static reward multiples: winners are repeatedly **truncated** before the TP surface is reached.
- Trailing, when tuned aggressively to “protect” open profit, behaves like a **high-pass filter** on price path: it realizes small wins that do not finance the loss tail created by false continuation breaks.

**Why this configuration underperforms (mechanism).** On XAUUSD M5, continuation is frequently **medium horizon** but **low persistence** relative to the noise bandwidth. Aggressive trailing assumes that favorable excursion is **monotone** once initiated; the tape exhibits **partial mean reversion inside trends** (local adverse excursions) that trigger premature trail tightening.

**Structural conclusion.** **Gold M5 is not a high-persistence trend market** in the sense required by “pure trend + static RR + aggressive profit lock.” The correct institutional analogy is **harvesting interrupted continuation**, not **riding smooth trends**.

---

### Test B — Pure Mean Reversion v2 (continuation suppressed; MR dominant; low agreement)

**Design intent.** Stress-test whether MR alone can substitute for continuation edge when continuation engines are effectively removed or heavily deprioritized and consensus/agreement is relaxed to increase fill rate.

**Observed behavior.**

- Profitability **deteriorates materially**.
- The **continuation edge disappears** from the portfolio: what remains is a mixture of MR fills on a tape where MR is structurally **contaminated** by sweep-and-revert microstructure.

**Why this configuration fails (mechanism).** MR profitability on XAUUSD is not a standalone “mode”; it is often **a phase inside a broader continuation failure** (liquidity grab → reversal). Without continuation engines, the system cannot monetize the dominant **impulse–retrace–continuation** cadence; MR becomes a **noise amplifier** under volatility clustering.

**Structural conclusion.** **Mean reversion standalone is insufficient** for the documented XAUUSD M5 structural profile (see profile section below). MR may exist as a **satellite** module, not as the primary identity of the engine.

---

### Test C — Ultra Pure Continuation (tight alignment, enlarged TP, lengthened continuation extraction)

**Design intent.** Push reward extraction to the right tail: larger TP multiples and longer “allowed continuation” before exit discipline engages, to test whether insufficient persistence in Test A was purely an **exit-too-soon** artifact.

**Observed behavior.**

- **Average profit increases** (right-shift of the conditional winner distribution).
- **Net profitability remains negative** (integrated expectancy does not cross zero).

**Why this configuration still fails (mechanism).** Enlarging TP does not remove **adverse excursion risk**; it reallocates probability mass from “small win” to “medium loss / timeout loss.” On M5 gold, **continuation exists** but is **not stable enough** for ultra-trend exploitation without **regime conditioning**: the enlarged TP increases **path variance** without a corresponding reduction in **bad regime participation**.

**Structural conclusion.** Continuation is **real but conditionally sparse**; “ultra continuation” without **bad regime removal** is mathematically equivalent to **buying optionality without pricing the negative drift regimes**.

---

### Test D — Contextual Continuation (selective continuation; momentum confirmation; stricter context)

**Design intent.** Reintroduce selectivity: continuation only when contextual gates agree, aiming to reduce participation in chop and sweep-dominated intervals.

**Observed behavior.**

- **Drawdown improves** (path risk compresses).
- **Trade quality improves** (higher conditional win quality / lower toxic fill rate proxy).
- **Profit factor remains < 1** (integrated edge still negative).

**Why this is informative (mechanism).** Contextual filters improve **stability** by removing a slice of the worst participation, but they do not, by themselves, solve **left-tail loss mass** created by (i) exit geometry mismatch and (ii) residual exposure to **bad volatility regimes** where even “good” entries degrade.

**Structural conclusion.** **Context improves stability**, but **does not eliminate bad volatility regimes**; the next marginal dollar of alpha is not in “more context” alone—it is in **regime-aware exit and regime exclusion**.

---

### Test E — Adaptive Reward Extraction (adaptive trailing; tighter SL; realistic TP; increased breathing room)

**Design intent.** Jointly optimize **risk cap per trade** (SL distance), **profit capture** (TP realism), and **continuation financing** (breathing room) while allowing trailing to adapt rather than statically choke winners.

**Observed behavior.**

**Why this matters (quant interpretation).** A PF near **1.0** under realistic tick modeling is strong evidence that the system is operating close to a **market equilibrium surface** for the chosen participation set: you are no longer “missing alpha” in the sense of a broken entry model; you are **paying frictions and bad regimes** that dominate the residual.

**Architectural conclusion.** **Reward extraction geometry is near-optimal** *conditional on the current participation set*. The remaining loss is less “model form error” and more **regime contamination** and **microstructure tax**.

### Test F — Accelerated Reward Locking (smaller SL; faster trailing; shortened continuation extraction)

**Structural conclusion.** **Execution geometry is extremely sensitive** to trailing speed, breathing room, and continuation duration on XAUUSD M5; there is no stable “set-and-forget” point without regime conditioning.

**Quantitative translation of “edge almost found”:** the edge is **conditional**—it exists in a subset of tape states—while the unconditional average remains dragged by **bad volatility regimes**. The correct engineering response is **state exclusion** and **adaptive geometry**, not global tightening.
